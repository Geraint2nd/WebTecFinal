Adobo 3 Ways

How to Make Adobo in 3 Ways
Filipino Adobo is a classic dish with countless variations. Ninong Ry, a popular Filipino chef, is known for his creative takes on traditional recipes. Here's how you can prepare Adobo in three ways inspired by his cooking style:

prep time

10 mins.

cook time

15-20 mins.

serving

4 servings

1. Classic Adobo (Soy Sauce and Vinegar)

    Ingredients:

  • 1 kg chicken (or pork belly)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup vinegar
  • 6 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 cup water

Step 1:

Sauté garlic until fragrant, add the meat, and brown it. Then, pour in the marinade, vinegar, and water. Simmer without stirring until the sauce thickens.

Step 2:

Serve hot over steamed rice. Enjoy your flavorful classic adobo!

2. Adobo sa gata

    Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken (cut into serving pieces) or pork (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1 head garlic, minced
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3-4 bay leaves
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (optional, for added umami)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil

Step 1:

Prepare the Meat: Use your choice of protein, typically chicken or pork. Cut it into chunks, ensuring they are ready for cooking.

Step 2:

Sauté Aromatics: In a large pan, sauté garlic, onion, and bay leaves in a bit of oil until fragrant.

Step 3:

Add Vinegar and Soy Sauce: Pour in the soy sauce and vinegar, then allow the mixture to simmer, letting the vinegar cook off for a few minutes.

Step 4:

Simmer with Coconut Milk: Add the coconut milk to the pan. Stir everything together and let it simmer on medium heat until the meat is tender and the flavors meld, creating a creamy sauce.

Step 5:

Season to Taste: Adjust the seasoning with salt, pepper, and other flavorings, such as fish sauce, to taste. Let the dish cook until the sauce has thickened to your liking.

3. Adobo sa Miso

    Ingredients:

  • 1 kg chicken or pork, cut into chunks
  • 2-3 tablespoons miso paste (yellow or red, depending on preference)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1 head garlic, minced
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3-4 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional, for balance)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1 cup water, or as needed

Step 1:

Sauté Aromatics: Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Sauté minced garlic, chopped onion, and bay leaves until fragrant.

Step 2:

Cook the Meat: Add the chicken or pork to the pan. Cook until lightly browned.

Step 3:

Add Miso Paste: Stir in the miso paste and coat the meat thoroughly. This enhances the umami flavor and integrates it into the dish.

Step 4:

Simmer with Soy Sauce and Vinegar: Pour in the soy sauce and vinegar. Let it simmer for a few minutes to reduce the rawness of the vinegar.

Step 5:

Add Water: Pour in water to deglaze the pan and create a sauce. Let it simmer until the meat becomes tender, about 30-40 minutes.

Step 6:

Adjust Seasoning: Taste the sauce and adjust with sugar, salt, or additional miso paste as needed to balance the flavors.

Step 7:

Thicken Sauce (Optional): If a thicker sauce is preferred, let it reduce further or mash some of the cooked meat or miso into the sauce.
