Chop Suey

How to Make Chop Suey
Ninong Ry's chop suey is a flavorful twist on the classic Filipino-Chinese stir-fry dish, showcasing his signature style of bold flavors, hearty ingredients, and no-fuss cooking techniques. Chop suey, a beloved staple in Filipino households, combines a medley of fresh, vibrant vegetables and tender proteins in a rich, umami-packed sauce. What sets Ninong Ry's version apart is his attention to balancing textures and flavors, ensuring that every bite is a satisfying mix of savory, sweet, and slightly smoky goodness.

prep time

5 minutes.

cook time

10-15 minutes.

serving

4 servings

instructions

step 1 : Prepare the ingredients:

  • Slice the meat and vegetables as listed above.
  • Mix the sauce ingredients (except cornstarch slurry) in a bowl.

    • step 2:Cook the protein:

  • Heat oil in a large wok or pan over medium-high heat.
  • Sauté garlic, onion, and ginger until fragrant.
  • Add pork or chicken and stir-fry until lightly browned.
  • Add shrimp and chicken liver (if using) and cook until just done. Set aside.

    • step 3:Grill

    change the oven setting to grill at 200°C 392°F6. Remove the ribs from the oven, place on a heatproof surface and discard the foil. Let the dish sit for 3 to 4 minutes and don't tip the marinade out. Use kitchen tongs to transfer each cooked rib to a silicon mat on a baking tray. Use a pastry brush to brush the marinade (leftover in the baking dish) onto each rib. Place the ribs into the oven on the top shelf. Grill them for 10 minutes, then remove and re-baste. Return the ribs to the oven for another 10 minutes of grill time.

    step 3: Serve

    once grilled remove the ribs from the oven and place the tray onto a heatproof surface. Let them sit for at least 2 minutes. Then use kitchen tongs to transfer them to a serving dish. Provide herbed yogurt sauce or parsley yogurt sauce for dunking.

    ingredients

    protein:

    200g chicken breast or thigh, sliced

    200g shrimp, peeled and deveined

    100g pork slices or bacon (optional)

    vegetables:

    1 medium carrot, sliced into thin diagonal pieces

    1 cup broccoli florets

    1 cup cauliflower florets

    1 medium red bell pepper, sliced into strips

    1 medium green bell pepper, sliced into strips

    1 cup young corn, sliced

    1 cup snap peas or snow peas

    1 small head of cabbage, chopped into large pieces

    sauce:

    3 cloves garlic, minced

    1 medium onion, chopped

    2 tbsp oyster sauce

    1 tbsp soy sauce

    1 tsp sesame oil

    1 cup chicken broth or water

    1 tbsp cornstarch dissolved in 2 tbsp water (for thickening)

    seasonings:

    Salt and pepper, to taste

    A pinch of sugar (optional)

    optional:

    Quail eggs, boiled and peeled

    Mushroom slices
