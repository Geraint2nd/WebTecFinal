Halo - Halo by Ninong Roy

How to Make Halo - halo
The best crane asada is lightly smoky, crispy in all the right places but tender in others, and redolent with the triple threat of spice, heat, and a hint of sweetness.

prep time

5 min.

cook time

5 min.

serving

2 servings

instructions

step 1 : Prepare the Ingredients

Sweeten the Fruits: If using fresh fruits like bananas and jackfruit, you can sweeten them by boiling with sugar until tender.

Make Leche Flan: Prepare the leche flan ahead of time and let it cool before cutting it into cubes.

Prepare Ube Halaya: If you don’t have store-bought ube halaya, you can make it by cooking grated purple yam with sugar and milk until thick.

Step 2: Assemble the Halo-Halo

Layer the base
  • In a large serving bowl, start by adding a layer of sweetened red beans at the bottom.
  • Follow with a layer of sweetened bananas and jackfruit.

    • Add shaved ice
  • Generously pile shaved ice on top of the fruit layers until it reaches the rim of the bowl.
    • Top with Milk and Flavors:
  • Pour evaporated milk over the shaved ice.
  • Add cubes of leche flan and dollops of ube halaya on top.
    • Finish with Toppings:
  • Add macapuno, buko, and pinipig on top for texture.
  • If desired, add a scoop of ice cream for extra creaminess.

    • step 3: Grill

    Heat outdoor grill, or a stovetop grill pan or large cast-iron skillet to high heat. Brush the grates or pan with olive oil.Using a pair of tongs, remove steak from marinade, and gently shake off any excess. Sear for 3–4 minutes on each side (resist the temptation to move it or check the crust—when the meat lifts away easily from the cooking surface, it’s ready) until cooked to preferred doneness. You’re looking for a crispy, charred exterior and perfectly cooked interior: with especially thin cuts of beef like skirt steak, aim for medium-rare with an internal temperature of 135 degrees Fahrenheit. Anything more, and these thinner cuts can get chewy and tough.

    step 4: Serve

    Remove from heat and place steak on a clean cutting board for 10–15 minutes to rest. This will allow all the juices to be reabsorbed before you slice. Slice the steak across the grain into thin slices and serve immediately.

    ingredients

    Base ingredients:

    Shaved Ice: Enough to fill your serving bowl

    Evaporated Milk: 1 cup (adjust to taste)

    Leche Flan: 1 piece, cut into small cubes

    Ube Halaya (Purple Yam Jam): 1 cup

    Sweetened Bananas: 1 cup, sliced

    Sweetened Jackfruit: 1 cup, sliced

    Sweetened Red Beans: 1 cup

    Macapuno (Coconut Sport): 1 cup

    Buko (Young Coconut): 1 cup, shredded or sliced

    Pinipig (Pounded Rice Flakes): for topping

    Optional ingredients for Variation:

    Ice Cream: Any flavor (ube or mango are popular choices)

    Fruit Cocktail: For additional flavor and texture
