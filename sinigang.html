How to Make Sinigang Baboy
Ninong Ry showcases his culinary creativity by preparing Sinigang in two distinct styles, emphasizing the versatility of this traditional Filipino dish. His recipes highlight the use of fresh ingredients and the signature sourness that defines Sinigang, whether made with pork or beef. In his engaging cooking videos, he demonstrates how to achieve rich flavors while keeping the process accessible for home cooks. Ninong Ry's approach not only celebrates Filipino cuisine but also inspires viewers to experiment with different variations of this beloved soup.

prep time

10 minutes.

cook time

30 minutes.

serving

4 servings

instructions:

Sinigang na Baboy (Pork Sinigang)
ingredients:
  • 1 kg pork belly or ribs
    •
  • 8 cups water
    •
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
    •
  • 2 medium tomatoes, quartered
    •
  • 1 radish (labanos), sliced
    •
  • 2 cups water spinach (kangkong)
    •
  • 2-3 green chili peppers (siling pangsigang)
    •
  • 1 packet of Sinigang mix (or fresh tamarind for sourness)
    •
  • Salt and pepper to taste
    •

    step 1 : Boil the Pork:

    In a large pot, add the pork and water. Bring to a boil and skim off any impurities that rise to the surface.

    step 2: Add Aromatics:

    Add the onion and tomatoes. Let it simmer for about 30 minutes or until the pork is tender.

    step 3: Incorporate Vegetables

    Add the radish and cook for another 5 minutes.

    step 4: Add Sourness:

    Stir in the Sinigang mix or fresh tamarind until fully dissolved.

    step 5: Finish with Greens:

    Add the water spinach and green chili peppers. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until the greens are wilted.

    step 6: Season:

    Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper to taste.

    Sinigang na Baboy (Pork Sinigang)
    ingredients:
  • 1 kg beef shank or short ribs
    •
  • 8 cups water
    •
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
    •
  • 2 medium tomatoes, quartered
    •
  • 1 cup eggplant, sliced
    •
  • 1 cup long green beans (sitaw)
    •
  • 2 cups water spinach (kangkong)
    •
  • 2-3 green chili peppers (siling pangsigang)
    •
  • 1 packet of Sinigang mix (or fresh tamarind for sourness)
    •
  • Salt and pepper to taste
    •

    step 1 : Prepare the Beef:

    In a pot, combine beef and water. Bring to a boil, skimming off any foam that forms.

    step 2: Add Vegetables:

    Once boiling, add onion and tomatoes, then simmer for about an hour or until tender.

    step 3: Add Eggplant and Beans:

    Introduce eggplant and long green beans into the pot, cooking for another 5 minutes.

    step 4: Sour It Up:

    Mix in the Sinigang mix or fresh tamarind until well blended.

    step 5: Finish with Greens:

    Add water spinach and green chili peppers, cooking for an additional few minutes.

    step 6: Season to Taste:

    Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper to taste.
